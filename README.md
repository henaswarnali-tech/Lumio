##  What Is LUMIO?

**LUMIO** is a free, offline-capable, gamified self-learning mobile platform for underserved children and adults worldwide — from zero literacy to fully employable skills.

Millions of people are locked out of quality education not because they lack intelligence, but because the system was never built for them. Child labourers, rural communities, neurodivergent learners, adults who never finished school — every platform that claims to serve them still assumes they have internet, a teacher, and a brain that works one specific way.

LUMIO was built for everyone that assumption leaves behind.

-  **Mobile-first** — works on any basic Android smartphone
-  **Offline-capable** — download once, learn forever
-  **Gamified** — designed like a game, not a textbook
-  **Neuro-inclusive** — adapts to ADHD, dyslexia, low literacy, and neurotypical learners
-  **Adaptive placement** — every learner starts at their own level automatically
-  **Zero to skilled** — from absolute zero literacy to professional employable skills
-  **Blockchain-verified certificates** — tamper-proof credentials recognised globally
-  **Fully open source** — forever free, forever community-owned

It would start wherever the learner starts — no prerequisites, no shame about being behind. It would adapt to how their brain works. It would teach things that are directly useful in real life. And it would end with something the learner can actually show someone — not just a certificate, but a portfolio of real work, in a world that increasingly wants evidence not credentials.

That's LUMIO.

## The Scale of What We're Trying to Address

Over 300 million children are currently out of school. 750 million adults cannot read or write. But the crisis isn't just about the children and adults who are entirely outside the system. It's also about the hundreds of millions who are nominally inside it — who attend school sporadically, who passed through without learning to read properly, who studied for years and graduated without skills that connect to actual employment.

Existing free platforms — and there are good ones — still require connectivity, still require baseline literacy, still tend to measure success in the way that made sense for students who already had most of what they needed. LUMIO is trying to serve the people those platforms reach last, if at all.

## How It Works

LUMIO is a self-learning app. Students learn alone, at their own pace, with no teacher required — though it works well alongside human educators too.

```
STAGE 0 — PLACEMENT
    Five minutes of adaptive questions
    Student starts at their own level — no wrong answer, no judgment

STAGE 1 — FOUNDATION  (Grades 1–5)
    From zero literacy to critical thinking, digital skills, and life skills
    English taught alongside every home language, from Grade 1
    Ethics, emotional intelligence, body safety, and financial literacy throughout

            ★ Student chooses their pathway ★

STAGE 2 — PATHWAYS
    Technical · Business · Creative · Health · Agriculture · Education
    5 levels per pathway, each producing real portfolio work
    Choose one or two — two pathways unlock a merged specialisation

            ★ Student chooses their career direction ★

STAGE 3 — PROFESSIONAL SKILLS
    Short, specific, employer-ready courses
    Blockchain-verified certificate on completion

GRADUATION
    Verified transcript · Graduate portfolio · Scholarship-ready
    Employment-ready OR university application-ready — or both
```

Two genuine choice points. One at the end of Grade 5, where students choose their pathway based on what interests them. One at the end of each pathway, where students choose between self-employment, job-seeking, and entrepreneurship — three genuinely different life directions that require different preparation. Most education systems only prepare students for one of those. LUMIO teaches all three.

## What's Actually in the Curriculum

The full curriculum with research foundations and assessment philosophy is in [CURRICULUM.md](CURRICULUM.md).  Here is the honest summary of what it covers and why.

**Foundation Grades 1–5** teach literacy, numeracy, and English simultaneously — alongside emotional intelligence, body safety, ethics, and financial thinking. Every grade builds on the last. No grade skips any foundation. A child entering at Grade 1 with zero literacy receives everything they need, in sequence, to reach Grade 5 ready to choose a meaningful pathway.

**The six pathways** go deeper into Technical, Business, Creative, Health, Agriculture, and Education fields. Each pathway teaches the skills of the field, the ethics of that field, the multiple income streams available within it, and the AI tools relevant to it. Each ends with preparation for all three career directions. Each produces real work for a student's portfolio — not school projects, but things that can be shown to employers, universities, and scholarship committees.

**Woven throughout everything:**

*Ethics* — not as a separate subject but in every grade and pathway. Business ethics, digital ethics, environmental ethics, medical ethics, historical ethics. The reasoning: ethical judgment is a skill, and like all skills it develops through practice across varied contexts, not through a single class.

*Multiple income streams* — the explicit, repeated teaching that one income source is one point of failure. What active income is. What passive income actually takes to build. How to build a second income stream alongside the first. Real examples from people in similar circumstances who did it.

*English* — mandatory in every language version, from Grade 1 onward, progressing from basic greetings to academic and professional fluency by graduation. English is the language of the global labour market and international higher education. Withholding it from learners because they speak a different language is a form of gatekeeping. LUMIO teaches both the student's native language and English simultaneously.

*Creativity as an economic skill* — not just in the Creative pathway. Taught as the thinking skill most resistant to automation across every pathway. Specific techniques: brainstorming, lateral thinking, iteration, creative confidence, creativity under constraints. Every simulation in LUMIO has multiple correct answers because that's what the real world looks like.

*Biodiversity and climate justice* — taught as science and ethics simultaneously. The science without the justice is incomplete. The justice without the science is ineffective. Students learn what biodiversity actually is and why it matters to food systems, not just to nature. They learn who caused climate change, who is suffering most from it, and how to act on that understanding without being paralysed by it.

*AI literacy* — what AI actually is, what it can do, what it cannot reliably do, and which jobs it will and won't replace. Students who understand this can use AI as a tool. Students who don't understand it are more likely to be replaced by people who do.

*Behaviour with others* — taught explicitly, from Grade 1. How to behave with peers, with adults, with people different from you, and with animals. Respectful disagreement is not disrespect. Cruelty to animals is ethically wrong. Including someone who is being left out is a choice that requires practice.

*Scholarships* — because most scholarship information assumes you have a school counsellor, know where to look, and have the English to navigate the application. LUMIO provides what students from vulnerable communities actually need: a full table of major international scholarships, what each requires, and exactly how LUMIO prepares students for each requirement. Because the students who overcame the most have the most compelling stories — if they know how to tell them.

## Student Choice — Really

The two choice points in LUMIO are not token moments. They are the structural design of the whole thing.

At the end of Grade 5, every student sees a pathway selection screen showing real people from communities like theirs who followed each pathway and where it led. Not aspirational models from wealthy countries. People they might recognise. Students choose based on what interests them, what they're good at, and where they want to go. They can choose two pathways if they want to — and finishing two unlocks a merged specialisation.

| Pathway | Merged with | Becomes |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Technical | Creative | Digital Product Designer |
| Business | Technical | Tech Entrepreneur |
| Health | Education | Community Health Educator |
| Creative | Business | Creative Entrepreneur |
| Agriculture | Business | AgriPreneur |
| Technical | Education | EdTech Builder |

At the end of each pathway, students choose a career direction. This is where LUMIO is most deliberately different from most education:

**Self-Employment** means you sell your own skill directly. You are the business. The freedom is real. The income ceiling is also real — it stops when you stop working. LUMIO teaches you how to move beyond that ceiling over time.

**Job-Seeking** means working within someone else's organisation. This is the most accessible path for many students immediately after LUMIO. It provides stability and a starting point. LUMIO teaches it without romanticising it — worker rights exist, building additional income streams alongside employment is wise, and the skills to advance within an organisation are learnable.

**Entrepreneurship** means building a system that can eventually earn without you personally doing every task. The highest potential of the three. The highest risk. Most businesses fail, and LUMIO is honest about why and how to reduce those odds.

Students can choose more than one direction. The most financially resilient people often combine all three — employment for stability, freelance work for income diversity, and something they're slowly building. LUMIO teaches this combination explicitly, because it's wisdom that doesn't get passed down to people who didn't grow up around financially successful adults.

## What Students Graduate With

Every LUMIO graduate leaves with six things:

**Blockchain-verified certificates** — one per completed grade, one per completed pathway level, one per Stage 3 course. Each has a QR code for instant verification by any employer or university anywhere in the world. Tamper-proof. Cannot be inflated.

**Verified graduate transcript** — every course completed, every skill demonstrated, every simulation passed, with dates and mastery levels. Formatted for employer review and university applications.

**Graduate portfolio** — real work created throughout: essays, business plans, design projects, code samples, health education materials, agricultural plans. Hosted on a permanent LUMIO portfolio page. Employers and universities can see what the student made, not just what they passed.

**Employment readiness** — professional CV built in the app, cover letter templates in native language and English, practised interview responses for their chosen field, knowledge of their rights as a worker, and a personal plan for building multiple income streams.

**University and scholarship readiness** — for students who completed the relevant modules: a personal statement draft, academic writing samples, and preparation for scholarship applications to programmes like Chevening, Fulbright, Erasmus Mundus, Commonwealth, and others.

**Global opportunity access** — professional English, knowledge of how to access the remote work market internationally, understanding of international payment systems, and a digital professional presence. These are the specific keys to doors the global economy has opened but that most people in vulnerable communities don't know exist.

## Built for Every Brain

LUMIO adapts to four learner profiles, selected during onboarding through eight visual questions. No diagnosis required. Change modes anytime.

| Mode | What Changes |
|------|-------------|
| ADHD | 3-minute sessions · reward every 2 questions · max 3 screen elements · break reminders every 15 minutes |
| Dyslexia | OpenDyslexic font · wider spacing · warm background · word-by-word audio highlighting |
| Low Literacy | Icon-first navigation · audio instructions throughout · tap to hear before tap to answer |
| Neurotypical | Standard experience |

The modes are based on evidence — what the research on ADHD, dyslexia, and early literacy actually says, not guesswork. And they're a starting point, not a final word. We need neurodiversity specialists to review and improve them. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

## Designed Around Child Safety

Many LUMIO users don't have a safe adult in their lives. "Tell a trusted adult" is incomplete advice when the trusted adult is the problem, or doesn't exist.

LUMIO's approach is different. Students learn a trust ladder: name what you're feeling to yourself first, write it in a private journal if nothing else, tell one person outside your immediate situation if you safely can, access a helpline if one exists and is safe in your location, and keep this knowledge for when you're older and in a safer situation.

The private journal is stored only on the device. Never synced. Never accessible to anyone else. One gentle offer after sensitive lessons. No pressure. No consequences either way.

LUMIO does not use AI chat to respond to trauma disclosures or crisis statements. This is a permanent design decision. The appropriate response to a child disclosing harm is human connection and appropriate referral — not an automated chat response.

For the full reasoning and an open invitation to child psychologists and trauma specialists to review and improve this approach: [CHILD_SAFETY_PSYCHOLOGY.md](CHILD_SAFETY_PSYCHOLOGY.md)

## How It Stays Free

Students use LUMIO completely free. Forever. No exceptions, no hidden costs, no ads.

LUMIO sustains itself through the people and organisations that serve students, never from the students themselves:

**Educator Accounts** — Teachers, NGO workers, and community facilitators managing groups of students pay a small monthly fee for a group dashboard. Their students are always free.

**Community Deployment** — Governments and organisations deploying LUMIO for a specific community — with local language, local content, and local branding — pay for deployment and support.

**Donations and Sponsorships** — Individuals and organisations who believe in what this is trying to do can support development directly. No advertiser ever influences what LUMIO teaches.

## Languages

LUMIO launches in **English and Bengali.**

Every other language is added by community contributors who speak that language and understand the communities it serves. We don't promise languages we can't deliver — it would be dishonest, and the communities waiting deserve better than false timelines.

English is mandatory in every language version, taught alongside the student's native language from Grade 1. This is not negotiable. See the curriculum for the reasoning.

## How To Contribute

LUMIO cannot be built by one person. Here is what it needs:

**Curriculum writers** — write or improve lesson content, simulations, and assessment questions. The curriculum is CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 and the full framework is in [CURRICULUM.md](CURRICULUM.md).

**Translators** — every language version opens LUMIO to a new community. Translation is not just language — it's cultural adaptation of examples, references, and local history content.

**Designers** — illustrations, animations, UI design. The design language is warm, human, and game-inspired. Lottie and Rive skills are particularly useful.

**Developers** — Flutter/Dart. See [SETUP.md](SETUP.md) for getting the development environment running.

**Child psychologists and trauma specialists** — the child safety content needs expert review. This is urgent and important. See [CHILD_SAFETY_PSYCHOLOGY.md](CHILD_SAFETY_PSYCHOLOGY.md).

**First aid instructors** — the Health pathway's first aid content needs review by qualified instructors before deployment.

**Developmental psychologists** — the Education pathway's child development content needs expert review.

**Educators** — review curriculum content, test simulations, identify gaps between what's written and what actually works with real learners.

**Testers** — test with real learners from the communities LUMIO is designed for. Report what works, what doesn't, and what's missing.

**Advocates** — share LUMIO with communities, NGOs, governments, and organisations that need it.
