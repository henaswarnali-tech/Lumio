# Code of Conduct

## The Short Version

LUMIO is built for some of the most vulnerable people in the world. The community that builds it should reflect the dignity and respect we're trying to give them.

Be kind. Be honest. Be useful. Don't make this space harder to be in for anyone who belongs here.

---

## Who This Applies To

Everyone who participates in the LUMIO community — in GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, commit messages, and anywhere else the project exists. This includes contributors, maintainers, reviewers, commenters, and anyone else present in the community spaces.

---

## What We Expect

**Be respectful.** Disagree with ideas, not people. A contribution being wrong or incomplete is not a reason to be unkind to the person who made it. Every contributor was a beginner once.

**Be honest.** If you don't know something, say so. If a decision has trade-offs, name them. If something isn't working, say it clearly and constructively. Honest communities build better things than polite ones.

**Be specific.** Vague criticism is not useful feedback. If something needs to change, explain what, why, and ideally how. The person receiving the feedback should be able to act on it.

**Be patient.** Many contributors are learning as they go. Many contributors are not native English speakers. Many are contributing in their spare time after long days. Give people room.

**Give credit.** If you build on someone else's idea or work, acknowledge it. If someone opened an issue that led to a fix, mention it. Small acknowledgements build a community where people feel their contributions matter.

**Protect the students.** LUMIO serves children and vulnerable adults. Any content, decision, or discussion that could put those users at greater risk — even indirectly — should be raised immediately and treated seriously.

---

## What We Won't Tolerate

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks in any form
- Dismissing or belittling contributions based on the contributor's background, experience level, country, language, or identity
- Sexist, racist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or otherwise discriminatory language or behaviour
- Publishing someone's private information without their consent
- Deliberate misrepresentation of others' words or intentions
- Sustained disruptive behaviour after being asked to stop

---

## If Something Goes Wrong

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this code of conduct, you can report it by opening a private issue or contacting the project maintainer directly through GitHub.

Reports will be taken seriously and handled with discretion. You will not be penalised for reporting in good faith.

The maintainer will review the situation and respond — typically within a week. Outcomes may include a private conversation, a public warning, temporary suspension from the repository, or permanent removal, depending on the severity and pattern of behaviour.

---

## For Maintainers

Maintainers are held to the same standard as contributors — and to a higher one when it comes to modelling the behaviour the community expects. A maintainer who is dismissive, unkind, or inconsistent in applying this code undermines the whole thing.

Enforcement should be proportionate, consistent, and documented. When a decision is made about conduct, the reasoning should be recorded somewhere the maintainer can refer back to it.

---

## A Note on Disagreement

Good projects have disagreements. Healthy disagreements about technical decisions, curriculum choices, and design direction are normal and productive. This code of conduct does not require agreement — it requires that disagreement happens with respect for the people involved.

The goal is a community where the best idea wins because it was argued most clearly — not because the person arguing it was loudest, most senior, or most intimidating.
