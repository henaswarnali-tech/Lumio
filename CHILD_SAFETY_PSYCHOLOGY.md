# LUMIO — Child Safety and Trauma-Informed Design Guidelines
### For Qualified Contributors: Child Psychologists, Trauma Specialists, and Child Safety Experts

---

## Who This Document Is For

This document is written by and for:

- Child psychologists and clinical psychologists with experience in childhood trauma
- Child protection specialists and social workers
- Trauma-informed educators and therapists
- Researchers in child development, abuse prevention, and recovery
- Organisations with direct experience supporting abused or at-risk children

If you have relevant qualifications and want to contribute to how LUMIO handles sensitive content for vulnerable children — **your expertise is needed and deeply welcomed.**

---

## Why This Document Exists

LUMIO serves children in some of the most vulnerable circumstances in the world — child labourers, children in conflict zones, children experiencing domestic violence, children without any adult they can safely trust.

The curriculum team has made foundational design decisions based on published research. But research is not the same as clinical experience. The people who have sat with traumatised children, who have seen what helps and what harms, who understand the nuance that no paper fully captures — those people need to shape how LUMIO handles sensitive content.

We do not have all the answers. We are asking for help.

---

## The Core Problem We Are Trying To Solve

LUMIO teaches:
- Body safety and consent
- Healthy and unhealthy relationships
- Mental wellbeing and emotional regulation
- Boundaries and personal rights

These topics are essential. They are also potentially harmful if taught without care to children who are currently experiencing abuse, neglect, or trauma.

**The tension:**
- Teaching this content can give children language, tools, and a seed of belief in their own worth
- Teaching this content carelessly can increase shame, distress, or helplessness in children who cannot change their circumstances

We need expert guidance on how to walk this line correctly.

---

## Current Design Decisions — Please Review and Challenge These

These are the decisions we have made so far. We believe they are sound. We may be wrong. Please challenge anything that your clinical experience contradicts.

### Decision 1 — Empowerment Frame Only
We teach body safety as "your body belongs to you" not "bad things can happen to your body."
We teach boundaries as strength, not as a response to threat.

**Question for experts:** Is this framing sufficient? Are there cases where naming the threat directly is more protective than this approach?

### Decision 2 — The Trust Ladder
Instead of "tell a trusted adult" — which assumes a safe adult exists — we teach a ladder:
1. Name it to yourself
2. Write it down privately
3. Tell one person outside your home if you can
4. Access a helpline if one exists and is safe
5. Keep this knowledge and use it when you are older and safer

**Question for experts:** Is Level 5 — "wait until you are older and safer" — ever harmful? Does it risk normalising an abusive situation for too long?

### Decision 3 — No In-App Reporting or AI Chat for Sensitive Disclosures
LUMIO deliberately does NOT have an AI conversation feature for sensitive disclosures, and does NOT have an in-app abuse reporting button that contacts authorities.

Reasons:
- AI responses to trauma disclosures can cause harm if not expertly designed
- In many countries, contacting authorities makes a child's situation worse not better
- We do not have the infrastructure to respond safely to disclosures

**Question for experts:** Is this the right decision? What is the minimum safe infrastructure needed before any form of disclosure support should be offered?

### Decision 4 — Private On-Device Journal
After sensitive lessons, LUMIO gently offers a private journal — stored only on the device, never synced, never read by anyone including developers.

**Question for experts:** Is a private journal beneficial or could it create risk — for example, if a caregiver finds it on the device?

### Decision 5 — No Mention of Local Authorities in Countries With Poor Child Protection
In countries without functional child protection system - LUMIO does not suggest contacting police or government authorities as a safety option.

**Question for experts:** How should we communicate the absence of safe reporting options without increasing hopelessness?

### Decision 6 — Content Warning Before Sensitive Lessons
Before body safety and relationship safety lessons, students see:
*"This lesson talks about keeping yourself safe. Some of it might bring up big feelings. You can take a break anytime or come back to it later."*

No opt-out is required — the student simply continues or leaves. No lesson is mandatory.

**Question for experts:** Is opt-out important? Should some content require active consent to proceed?

## Areas Where We Need Expert Contribution

### 1. Age-Appropriate Trauma Language
We need guidance on exactly what language is appropriate at each age level for discussing:
- Physical abuse
- Emotional abuse and neglect
- Sexual abuse (without graphic content)
- Domestic violence in the home

What words are safe? What words can re-traumatise? At what age can each concept be introduced?

### 2. Cultural Sensitivity in Abuse Content
Physical punishment of children is culturally normalised in many countries where LUMIO will be used. How do we:
- Teach that children deserve not to be hurt
- Without making children feel their parents are bad people
- Without creating conflict that could worsen the child's situation at home
- While still planting the seed that this is not okay

### 3. Helpline Database by Country
We need qualified contributors to research and verify:
- Which helplines and support organisations in each country are genuinely child-safe
- Which organisations have good track records vs poor ones
- What is the right format for presenting this information to a child who may be at risk

### 4. Indicators of Distress in App Behaviour
Without creating an intrusive monitoring system, are there ethical ways to design the app so that a child showing signs of significant distress might be gently offered support resources? Where is the line between helpful and surveillance?

### 5. Handling Disclosure Within The App
If we ever build any form of support feature — what is the minimum safe standard for handling a child's disclosure? What training, infrastructure, and safeguards must be in place first?

### 6. Post-Lesson Emotional Recovery
After a difficult lesson, what does good design look like? How should the app transition the child back to a regulated state before continuing to other content?

## How To Contribute Your Expertise

### Option 1 — Open an Issue
Go to the Issues tab of this repository and open a new issue with the label `child-safety-expert`.
Describe your background and the specific guidance you want to offer.
The maintainer will respond and we will work together to incorporate your input.

### Option 2 — Submit a Pull Request
If you want to directly edit this document or the curriculum, fork the repository, make your changes, and submit a Pull Request.
Please include your qualifications and the evidence base for your recommendations in the pull request description.

### Option 3 — Join the Advisory Group
If you or your organisation would like to be formally acknowledged as a child safety advisor to LUMIO, please open an issue with the label `advisory-request`.
Advisors are credited in the app and in all published documentation.
This is a volunteer role — there is no payment — but it is a real and meaningful contribution to a platform that will reach millions of children.

## What We Promise To Contributors

- Your expertise will be taken seriously and implemented thoughtfully
- You will be credited by name or organisation in the app and documentation
- No changes to child safety content will be made without consulting this advisory group once it is established
